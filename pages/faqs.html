FAQs

Here is some questions that you as a potential apprentice may want to ask:

How long does the apprenticeship last?

18-24 months.

Is there much homework?

You will often be assigned homework weekly to complete for the next lecture.

What modules make up the course?

There are 2 modules that make up the apprenticeship course:
- Knowledge Module 1: Software Development Methodologies
- Knowledge Module 2: Software Languages

How is the course assessed?

It will be assessed by an exam invigilator from the British Computer Society (BCS) by interview and discussion about your
submitted portfolio showing work you have done throughout your apprenticeship.

How often do I attend college?

1 day per week during the school term.

Are there a lot of essays for homework?

You will have the opportunity to work with different applications such as Microsoft applications to produce and display work.

Do I get to develop a piece of software or website?

Yes, you get to work on a personal software project and a chance to work as team to develop a functioning website for the college.

How long are the lectures for?

The lectures will last 6 hours.

What are the entry requirements for the course?

There is not set requirements as the employer sets their requirements, however it'll typically include one or more of the following:
- A Levels
- Level 3 Apprenticeship Completion
- Other relevant qualifications or experience
- Aptitude test with a focus on functional maths

Do I need to be able to understand coding?

Its not essential to understand coding to be successful on this course but it is beneficial.
